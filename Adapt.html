The Menu

For the Adapt Project, our focus was on aid. The product required changing a pre-existing idea and turning it into something that the visually impaired could use as well.

Problem Statement

The method of ordering food (in locations that provide menus) for the visually impaired can be inefficient.

Often times Restaurants may mot have braille menus because the process to make one takes too long and may cost around $125. The servers are in a rush to serve other customers which may ruin the experience of the visually Impaired.

Research

Interviewing one person, when ordering, he usually had to ask someone else to order for him. On behalf of the person accompanying the visually impaired person, they found it a hassle to order for this person, which ends up in having to guess for a meal to order for the visually impaired.Another problem we discovered was that not all visually impaired people know how to read in braille as stated from The National Federation for the Blind’s Article, “Fewer than 10 percent of the 1.3 million people who are legally blind in the United States are Braille readers. Further, a mere 10 percent of blind children are learning it.”This problem has been consistent throughout many years and hasn’t been widely discussed amongst the population due to the lack of visually impaired speaking out about the subject. The problem seems to be affecting most restaurants and visually impaired individuals increasing the time to serve,order, as well as Expense braille menus.

Summary

After figuring out the pros and cons of physical verses technological devices, we decided to focus on the technological design.We had to keep in mind the different applications that are helpful to the visually impaired and the ways they operate. For our solution, an auditory, voice command menu application would be the most effective.
